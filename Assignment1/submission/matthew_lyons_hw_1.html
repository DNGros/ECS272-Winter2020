"Light Speed - Fast, but Slow" by Dr. James O'Donoghue

Presented by Matthew Lyons for ECS 272

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nQUwHdSAhmw

This visualization is a video, and can be interacted with by clicking the embedded video to make it play. It shows the time light takes to travel relative to the inner planets of our solar system.

I think this is a very good visualization, as it does a good job of showing how vast space is, with using the speed of light as a visual measuring tool. It is well known that the speed of light is the fastest anything can travel in the universe, and we see in the beginning of the video the representation of light zipping around the Earth at a frantic pace. However, in the next few clips, it shows the travel time of light just to get to Mars from Earth, and we see the light beam slowly creeping across the black emptiness of space. Next, it shows the beam traveling from the sun to Earth, and it is moving even slower across the dark backdrop.

This visualization tells a story about how big the universe really is. When even the fastest object in the universe takes this long to travel just within our solar system, it emphasizes how far apart things are in space, and how empty it is.
