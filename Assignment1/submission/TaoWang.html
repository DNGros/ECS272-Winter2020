The picture above shows the price of "Big Mac" in different countries, they call it "The Big Mac Index".
The visualization itself is an iteractive application that allows users to choose different currencies and
the price at different certain amount of time. However, the image I embedded above is only one possible
perspective of the iteractive application. The visualization utilized different colors to discriminate differences
in data. Both the graph and the map shows the difference between the price of "Big Mac" in the US and that of in
other countries. The smaller gaps were shown in yellow and larger gap were shown in red and blue, where red is for
significantly lower and blue is for significantly higher. The visualization uses both bar graph and world map to present
the number in different countries. Since it tried to show data in world-wide, choosing a map to show the data is very intuitive to the users, it is very straighforward. In my opinion, it is a good visualization, since it is self explanatory, and the
users and understand it with first sight. In addition, the visualization is also impactful, because the price the Big Mac
can show a country's agriculture cost, commoditties, labor, and even real-estate. I believe being meaningful is another
criteria of a good visualization.
